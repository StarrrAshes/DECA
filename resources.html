Competition Prep

Preparing for DECA Events

Tips and strategies to help you perform your best at DECA competitions.

Tip

Know Your Event Format

Each DECA event has specific requirements — role-plays, prepared presentations, written plans, or online simulations. Read the DECA Guide thoroughly to understand the format, scoring rubrics, and time limits for your chosen event.

Tip

Practice Role-Plays

For role-play events, practice thinking on your feet. You'll typically get 10 minutes of preparation time before a 10-minute presentation with a judge. Work with classmates to simulate the pressure of timed presentations.

Tip

Study the Career Cluster Exams

Many events include a career cluster exam worth up to 50% of your score. Use DECA's practice exams and study the knowledge areas for your cluster — marketing, finance, hospitality, or business management.

Reference

DECA Glossary

Quick reference for common DECA terms and abbreviations.

ICDC
International Career Development Conference — DECA's annual international competition, rotating between Orlando, Anaheim, and Atlanta.
CTSO
Career and Technical Student Organization — national student organizations focused on career-readiness education, of which DECA is one.
Cluster Exam
A multiple-choice test covering core knowledge in one of DECA's four career clusters: Marketing, Finance, Hospitality, or Management.
Role-Play
A competitive event where participants respond to a business scenario presented by a judge, simulating a real-world professional interaction.
Prepared Event
A competitive event (formerly Written Events) requiring advance preparation of a pitch deck or research paper before presenting to judges.
PI
Performance Indicators — specific skills and knowledge areas evaluated during competitive events, listed in the DECA Guide.
SLC
State Leadership Conference — a state-level DECA conference where members compete for the chance to advance to ICDC.
Emerging Leader Series
Non-competitive DECA activities focused on community service, chapter management, and personal leadership development.
The DECA Diamond
DECA's official logo, a diamond shape whose four inner points represent the core values: Competence, Innovation, Integrity, and Teamwork.
VBC
Virtual Business Challenge — online business simulation competitions where members manage virtual companies in categories like retail and sports.
