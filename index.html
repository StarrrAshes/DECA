Our Mission

Building Tomorrow's Business Leaders

DECA Inc. is a 501(c)(3) not-for-profit CTSOCareer and Technical Student Organization — a national student group focused on career-readiness education. It is authorized by the United States Congress, the U.S. Department of Education, and state, district, and international departments of education.

Through its Comprehensive Learning Program, DECA integrates into classroom instruction, applies learning through competitive events, connects to business via industry partnerships, and promotes community service. The organization's core values are competence, innovation, integrity, and teamwork.

What DECA Offers

Four Career Clusters

DECA's programs span four career clusters, providing students with hands-on experience and competition opportunities in each area.

Marketing

Marketing

Develop skills in advertising, branding, digital marketing, sports and entertainment marketing, and consumer behavior through real-world case studies and role-play competitions.

Finance

Finance

Explore accounting, business finance, financial analysis, and personal financial literacy. DECA's finance events prepare students for careers in banking, investment, and financial planning.

Hospitality

Hospitality & Tourism

Learn about hotel management, restaurant operations, travel and tourism planning, and event management. Students gain practical experience in one of the world's largest industries.

Management

Business Management & Administration

Build leadership, human resources, operations, and entrepreneurship skills. Management events teach students how to lead teams, solve problems, and grow organizations.

Step 1: Explore DECA

Start by learning what DECA offers. Visit deca.org to understand the four career clusters (Marketing, Finance, Hospitality, Management). Talk to your school's DECA advisor or business teacher about joining your chapter.

Step 2: Prepare for Competition

Choose a competitive event that matches your interests and strengths. Read the DECA Guide for your event's format and rubrics. Practice role-plays with classmates, study for cluster exams, and attend chapter meetings for preparation tips.

Step 3: Compete & Grow

Start at your district or state conference. Top performers advance to the International Career Development Conference (ICDC). Even if you don't advance, DECA develops leadership, public speaking, and business skills that benefit your career for life. Get involved in chapter activities, community service, and the Emerging Leader Series!
