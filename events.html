Competitions & Events

DECA's competitive events challenge members across role-plays, prepared presentations, and online simulations.

Competitive Events

Event Categories

DECA's competitive events are grouped into three broad categories. Within each, events are offered across the four career clusters: marketing, finance, hospitality, and management.

First-Year Members

Principles of Business Administration

Beginner

Designed for first-year DECA members enrolled in introductory business courses. Participants analyze a case scenario and present to a judge in a 10-minute role-play after 10 minutes of preparation. Combined with a Business Administration Core Exam.

Individual

Individual Series Events

Intermediate

DECA's largest competitive event category. Members demonstrate proficiency in a specific career area through a role-play with a judge, combined with a career cluster exam. Events are available in every cluster.

Teams of Two

Team Decision Making

Advanced

Teams of two analyze a business case study and present strategic recommendations to judges. They receive 30 minutes of preparation for a 15-minute presentation, combined with averaged cluster exam scores.

Research

Business Operations Research

Members (individually or in teams of up to three) design and conduct research on a topic that changes annually, then present their findings and strategic plan. Requires a written component and oral presentation.

Marketing

Integrated Marketing Campaigns

Teams of up to three create a comprehensive marketing initiative for an event, product, or service. For 2025–2026, the written component is now a pitch deck of up to 20 slides, combined with an oral presentation and marketing cluster exam.

Entrepreneurship

Entrepreneurship Events

Includes Innovation Plan, Start-Up Business Plan, and other events where members design plans to start, innovate, or grow a business. For 2025–2026, Innovation Plan and Start-Up Business Plan now begin competition at the district level.

Project Mgmt

Project Management Events

Members use project management skills to initiate, plan, execute, monitor, and close a project. Competitors focus on budgeting, risk assessment, timeline development, and performance measurement.

Sales

Professional Selling & Consulting

Participants demonstrate sales or consulting skills in a one-on-one role-play. They must communicate value, handle objections, and provide strategic advice to a potential client or buyer.

Finance

Personal Financial Literacy

Measures members' ability to apply reliable information and systematic decision-making to personal financial decisions, including budgeting, credit management, saving, and investing.

Simulation

Virtual Business Challenges (VBC)

Members operate web-based business simulations through Knowledge Matters software. Qualifying rounds are conducted online, with participants competing for chartered association, regional, and overall rankings. Simulations are available in eight areas: Accounting, Entrepreneurship, Fashion, Hotel, Personal Finance, Restaurant, Retailing, and Sports.

Finance

Stock Market Game

Teams develop and manage an investment portfolio, making all decisions about stock selection, buying, and selling. The goal is to increase the value of the beginning portfolio. For 2025–2026, the written component is now a pitch deck format.

Community

Chapter Team Events

Chapters plan, implement, and evaluate large-scale activities to make a positive impact in their schools and communities. These events engage all chapter members and demonstrate the collective impact of DECA chapters.

Retail

School-Based Enterprise (SBE)

Students operate real businesses within their schools — such as spirit shops or food service operations — and present how their enterprise functions. SBE events provide hands-on experience in retail or food operations management.

Leadership

Emerging Leader Series

A leadership-focused program at ICDC where thousands of members participate in workshops, networking, and training sessions. The series develops 21st-century skills in collaboration, communication, critical thinking, and creativity.

Chapter Campaigns

Chapter Campaigns & Activities

DECA provides chapter campaigns throughout the year, giving chapters ideas for activities — from community service projects to member recruitment drives. Each chapter adapts these to further the organization's mission locally.

Conferences

International Career Development Conference (ICDC)

ICDC is DECA's premier annual event. More than 23,000 members, advisors, and business professionals attend to compete, learn, and network. Over 10,000 members participate in competitive events as finalists from their state or chartered association.

The conference also features the Emerging Leader Series, the School-Based Enterprise Academy, Executive Officer Campaigns, Advisor Professional Learning, and an exhibit hall with more than 100 college and business exhibitors.

ICDC is generally held in April or May. The conference location rotates between three host cities: Anaheim, California; Atlanta, Georgia; and Orlando, Florida.

What's New

2025–2026 Season Updates

Key changes to DECA's Competitive Events Program for the current season.

📝

Prepared Events Renaming

The broad category formerly known as "Written Events" is now called "Prepared Events" to better reflect the nature of these competitions.

📊

Pitch Deck Format

For Innovation Plan, Start-Up Business Plan, Stock Market Game, and the three Integrated Marketing Campaign Events, the written component is now a pitch deck of up to 20 slides instead of the previous paper format.

📋

Revised Rubrics

Full implementation of revised rubrics for all competitive events. Check the updated DECA Guide for the latest scoring criteria and guidelines.
