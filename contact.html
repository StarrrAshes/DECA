FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about DECA.

Students join DECA through their school's chapter. If your school doesn't have a chapter, you can work with a teacher to start one. Membership requires enrollment in a marketing, finance, hospitality, or management course. Visit deca.org for more details.

No. While competitive events are a major part of DECA, members can also participate in the Emerging Leader Series, chapter activities, community service projects, and networking events without competing.

The High School Division is open to students in grades 9–12. The Collegiate Division is open to students at colleges and universities. Some Principles events are limited to first-year DECA members only.

Yes. To register for and attend any DECA educational conference, students must be official DECA members listed on a DECA roster. Chaperones and family members may register if they meet the conference criteria.

DECA's four core values are competence, innovation, integrity, and teamwork. These are represented by the four inner points of the DECA Diamond, the organization's official logo.
